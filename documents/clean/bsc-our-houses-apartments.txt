Important information about the Berkeley Student Cooperative housing options.
Cloyne Court is only open to UC Berkeley students and is substance-free and academically themed. Many houses have shared bedrooms, shared kitchens, shared bathrooms, and common spaces. Room assignments are made at the unit level.
Food is included at all houses during fall and spring and at some houses during summer. Workshift is 5 hours per week to help run the house. The page also describes individual houses and apartments such as Afro House, Casa Zimbabwe, Euclid Hall, Hillegass Parker House, Hoyt Hall, Kidd Hall, Kingman Hall, Lothlorien, Sherman Hall, Stebbins, The Convent, Wilde House, Wolf House, Fenwick, Northside, and Rochdale.
Afro House emphasizes Black culture, history, and community. Casa Zimbabwe is described as a student co-op with a long history and a strong community identity. Euclid Hall is smaller and cozy, with a high single-to-double room ratio. Hillegass Parker House is limited to graduate students and re-entry students age 25 or older.
Hoyt Hall is women-identifying only in fall and spring and co-ed in summer. Kidd Hall is one of the smallest houses, with a backyard, pool table, and TV. Kingman Hall sits in North Berkeley near the Rose Garden and is known for its historic character. Lothlorien serves vegetarian meals and emphasizes activism and art.
Person of Color Theme House (Castro) focuses on low-income, first-generation, immigrant, and marginalized students of color. Ridge House is a historic Tudor-style house with hidden stairways and a garden. Sherman Hall is ADA accessible and has DSP singles. Stebbins is described as diverse, with residents from many backgrounds.
The Convent serves graduate and re-entry students in a clean, quiet former convent. Wilde House welcomes LGBTQIA+ and queer students and aims to create an inclusive community. Wolf House is a short walk from campus and balances social life with quiet study space. Fenwick and Rochdale are apartment-style co-op options, while Northside is quieter and aimed at graduate and professional students.
